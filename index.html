The tuple object (pronounced “toople” or “tuhple,” depending on whom you ask) is roughly like a list that cannot be changed—tuples are sequences, like lists, but they are immutable, like strings. Functionally, they’re used to represent fixed collections of items: the components of a specific calendar date, for instance.
